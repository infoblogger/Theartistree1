The five activities that are being done the most online during Covid-19

The Internet has become the main source of refuge of many in the midst of the pandemic and the only channel to keep in touch with the outside world, as well as to enjoy a some social interaction. Today , concerts and other events are held virtually through multiple platforms designed to sustain the availability of events.

The Internet and, more specifically, social networks have become work and life tools that have seen their usage patterns change drastically. According to the Global Web Index found that today what people do most online is to search about coronavirus , which is 68 percent while listening to music at 58%, watching movies 49%, watch funny videos with 42% of the time, and in the 5th spot, play games on the cellphone with 40% out of all the time.

Generation Millennials, which includes those born between 1981 and 1996 They are the generation that most want to know about the virus that affects all four generations studied, which has 71% of this disease; then Generation X at 69%, Generation Z at 67 percent, and Boomers with 54 percent.

Maria Alexis Martinez, a psychologist at the Universidad de los Andes, explained that during the outbreak and the resulting panic, people are seeking actions and information that could bring them peace, as we're in a time of crisis filled with uncertainty, which it generates in the vast majority of people. issues like stress and anxiety in the event that you contract the covid virus in the wild and you are a victim, you should also consider to that the requirement to stay at home.

Another one that stands out from the rest is gaming online with a 40% growth in the number of activities players do as a result of covid. This number is confirmed by the increased game downloads to cell phones, which have reached as high as 500%.

Alberto Pardo, CEO of Adsmovil said it was "the consumption of video games surpassed that of music in Colombia and was positioned in the third category, after social networks and news" in a local study and concluded that it was women who are downloading video games most .

When it comes to activities on social media The focus is on reading news and articles which accounts for 48% of the users and staying in touch with their friends at 41%; listening to music with 39%, playing games with 32% and sharing their opinions with 28%.

In this category of social networks, it's the youngest who present the highest amount of news which is 54% for millennials and 49% in Generation Z.

 

The professor and analyst at Javeriana University, Mario Morales. Javeriana University, Mario Morales has stated that "social networks basically fulfill three purposes during the current crisis. The first is to give users entertainment, which is something basic that users are searching for But in this particular situation it's a lot more realizing that the people of today are in greater fear and anxiety which leads users to search for information to calm that down; This is the way the second function is born that is to serve as an outlet for catharsis. Thirdly, they perform a function that they weren't made for and that is to need for information. This is negative because, having to deal with a constant stream of fake news as well as the constant stream of fake news, anxiety, paranoia and fear shoot up during times of crisis. which the professionals already planned in advance".
